Ron Bronson

Information Architect. Service Designer. Speaker.

Bio


Ron Bronson is a cross-functional strategist and designer, focused primarily on user experience for digital products and websites. He's spent the bulk of his practice working on large-scale digital transformation. Over the past few years, he's been thinking aloud about the on the deliberate frictions imposed on users, embedded both in the real world and digital experiences.

Day-to-day, he's hands on with UX/UI copy, navigating content management systems, evangelizing digital governance, developing strategy, devising iterative user testing, advocating for users and stakeholders, or mapping experiences to devise ways to improve service delivery.

Ron is currently head of visual + cross-functional design at 18F. Previously, he was a municipal web manager. Prior, he led statewide digital strategy for a college system. Ron is also veteran of the U.S. Air Force. He's presented over 40 conference talks and workshops on three continents since 2014.

Ron Bronson has a coaching career spanning two decades across the collegiate, club, and scholastic levels around the country. Currently, he's head boys tennis coach at Oregon City High School. In 2004, he invented the sport of Tennis Polo.

As a player, he played one season at Monmouth College in Illinois, after a 4-year enlistment in the U.S. Air Force. During his lone season, he was a team captain, and played singles (4th, 5th, 6th) and doubles. He was a quarter-finalist at #6 singles at the 2003 Midwest Conference team tournament. In high school, Ron was a 4-time letter winner and a team captain at Plainfield (NJ) High School. Under the mentorship of ETA Hall of Famer and former state Coach of the Year Donald Van Blake, he was part of three NJSIAA Group IV state playoff teams, a team conference championship in 1997 (after a record setting 17-7 season); and individually named an all-area honorable mention selection at doubles.
